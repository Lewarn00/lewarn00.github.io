Hello! I am a recent gradute of the University of Chicago ('23), where I studied computer science (with a specialization in Machine Learning) as well as English Language and Literature. This past year, I used AI to create the biggest video ever made (5.8B pixels), an artwork called TODEM (check it out) that I built alongisde renowned digital artist Jason Salavon. To date, my AI research has primarily involved conditional image synthesis, and has lead me to working with a vast range of models from GANs to Stable Diffusion. Broadly, I'm interested in the contruction, exploration, and harnesing of abstract low-dimensional representations of high-dimensional data, wherever they may be. Below are some cool things I've made.

Below is an example of an early TODEM render using AI for image reconstruction.

TODEM (Tapestry of Decadent Meritocracy) is an enormous artwork that required a ton of experimenting with AI models. The artwork itself evoled alongside AI, as our first renders used GANs (like this one), while our later renders used newly developed Stable Diffusion models. The artwork is best described as a huge mosiac where every tile, along with being a standalone art piece by itself, combines to create a cohesive image.

Above is the 33rd frame of the first version of TODEM which was released to the public. It was made using stable diffusion, automatic1111, as well as a custom system of generating massive tiled videos that I invented and built.

Above is the 33rd frame of the most version of TODEM which was released to the public. It was made using a model called qr-code-monster, which basically just uses ControlNet with depth.
